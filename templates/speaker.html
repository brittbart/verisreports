How speaker scoring works. A verdict assigned to a claim is a statement about the claim's relationship to available evidence — not a characterization of {{ speaker.name }} as a person. Verdicts reflect whether attributed claims were accurate at the time of reporting. They do not aggregate into a judgment about the speaker's overall reliability.

Claims attributed to a speaker in articles ("{{ speaker.name }} said X") are evaluated on whether the attribution is accurate and the claim is supported by evidence. Wire-service reprints and claims originated by the outlet (not attributed) are excluded. Full methodology →

Disagree with a verdict? Every claim and verdict on this page links to its per-article evidence. If you believe a specific verdict was assigned in error, send the verdict ID and the basis for your dispute. Corrections are reviewed under Methodology {{ methodology_version }}.
