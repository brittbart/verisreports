Methodology

How Verum Signal works.

Documented methodology. Same standards applied to every outlet.

The rules
1

Independence rule

Two sources are only independent if they obtained information through genuinely different means. Five outlets citing the same wire report counts as one source.

2

Consensus exception

When five or more outlets consistently report the same claim without contradiction, a verdict may be assigned at reduced confidence.

3

Outlet claims vs attributed claims

We distinguish between claims the outlet makes directly and claims attributed to named speakers. A politician's claim is scored against the politician's record, not the outlet's.

4

No human editorial decisions

Every verdict is generated automatically using the same prompt, the same standards, applied identically to every outlet.

Evaluate claims on evidence —
not on who made them.
Verdict definitions

Eight possible verdicts.

Every claim lands in one of eight categories. Six contribute to the outlet's reliability score. Two (Opinion and Not verifiable) are excluded from scoring. The thresholds are fixed, not negotiable.

The number on each card is the verdict's weight in the outlet's reliability score. Positive weights push the score up; negative weights pull it down.

+1.0 Supported

Supported

Two genuinely independent sources confirm the claim.

+0.5 Plausible

Plausible

Consistent with one credible source but cannot be corroborated.

+0.5 Corroborated

Corroborated

Five or more outlets consistently report the claim without contradiction. Reduced confidence vs Supported.

−0.5 Overstated

Overstated

Core fact is real but framing is misleading or lacks critical context.

−1.0 Disputed

Disputed

At least one credible independent source contradicts the claim.

−1.5 Not supported

Not supported

Available evidence directly contradicts the claim.

Excluded from scoring
Excluded Opinion

Opinion

Editorial commentary, columns, or content explicitly framed as opinion. Excluded from outlet scoring — opinion is the wrong category for the question "is this accurate?", not the wrong kind of content.

Excluded from scoring
Excluded Not verifiable

Not verifiable

Cannot confirm — necessary sources are unavailable, paywalled, or the claim cannot be empirically tested. Excluded from outlet scoring because absence of evidence is not evidence of inaccuracy.
