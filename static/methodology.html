01

Core principles

Verum Signal evaluates claims on evidence, not on who made them. Political identity, outlet affiliation, and speaker profile play no role in how a verdict is assigned.

Evidence over alignment

No claim is graded on a curve based on the outlet that published it or the political tendency it supports. A claim is evaluated against what independent, credible sources report — nothing else.

Signals, not verdicts on people

Verum Signal scores outlet reporting accuracy, not speaker truthfulness. An outlet that accurately reports a false statement receives no penalty — the claim is excluded from scoring.

Documented and auditable

Every element of the pipeline — prompts, weights, formula constants, gate thresholds — is documented here and subject to revision through the public methodology changelog.

02

Verification pipeline

Articles are ingested from RSS feeds every three hours. Each article passes through the following stages before a verdict is assigned:

1
Ingestion & deduplication
Articles are pulled from monitored RSS feeds. Wire reprints are detected via byline analysis and excluded from outlet scoring.
2
Claim extraction
Factual claims are extracted from article text. Opinion and editorial content is classified and excluded at this stage.
3
Priority scoring
Claims are scored for verification priority. A 30-point threshold filters out low-signal claims (entertainment, lifestyle).
4
Breaking news gate
Claims about fast-moving events are held for a minimum 6-hour window before verification begins.
5
Evidence search & triangulation
The pipeline queries multiple independent sources. Anonymous sources cannot produce a supported verdict.
6
Verdict assignment
One of eight verdict labels is assigned based on evidence gathered, logged with pipeline version and timestamp.
7
Score computation
Verdicts are aggregated into an outlet score. not_verifiable and opinion are excluded from the denominator.
03

Verdict types

Eight verdict labels are used. Six carry scoring weight; two are excluded from the outlet score denominator.

supported+1.0Confirmed by two genuinely independent sources. Anonymous sources cannot produce this verdict.
plausible+0.5Consistent with available evidence; one credible source only. Not contradicted, but not independently confirmed.
corroborated+0.5Five or more outlets report consistently, no external contradicting source. No independent external check was possible.
overstated−0.5The core fact is real, but the framing, magnitude, or implication is exaggerated or misleading.
disputed−1.0At least one credible independent source directly contradicts the claim.
not_supported−1.5Evidence actively contradicts the claim. The strongest negative verdict.
not_verifiableexcl.Sources unavailable, paywalled, or claim cannot be empirically tested. Excluded from scoring.
opinionexcl.Editorial or opinion content. Excluded from outlet scoring.
04

Scoring formula

Outlet scores are computed from the distribution of scoreable verdicts. The formula normalises the weighted sum to a 0-100 range.

weighted_sum = sum(verdict_weight x verdict_count)
scoreable = verdicts excluding not_verifiable and opinion
normalised = (weighted_sum / scoreable + 1.5) / 2.5
score = min(max(normalised x 100, 0), 100)
Divisor fixed at 2.5 (v1.5). All not_supported scores 0; all supported scores 100.
High
70-100
Claims consistently well-supported by independent sources.
Medium
40-69
Mixed record — some well-supported, some contested or overstated.
Low
0-39
Frequent disputes or unsupported claims in the verified sample.
05

Outlet tiers

Outlets are assigned to one of four tiers based on total scoreable verdicts. Tier is displayed alongside every outlet score.

Excluded
<20
Insufficient data. No score displayed.
Limited Data

Stabilizing
50-99
Score becoming statistically meaningful.
Published
100+

Leaderboard note

The public leaderboard displays 95% bootstrap confidence intervals for Published-tier outlets. An outlet with 25 verdicts and a score of 72 is not meaningfully comparable to one with 200 verdicts and a score of 70.

06

Quote & attribution rules

Verum Signal scores outlet reporting accuracy, not speaker truthfulness.

Accurately reported false statement
If an outlet accurately quotes a speaker who made a false claim, the outlet is not penalised. The claim is excluded from outlet scoring.
Inaccurately reported statement
If an outlet misrepresents what a speaker said — through selective quotation, distortion, or false paraphrase — that is scored as a reporting failure.
Anonymous sources
Claims sourced exclusively from anonymous sources cannot receive a supported verdict. Maximum achievable is plausible.
Wire reprints
Articles identified as wire reprints via byline detection are excluded from outlet scoring.
07

Breaking news gate

In v1, all claims pass through a static 6-hour gate before verification begins. Claims published within the last 6 hours are held until the window closes.

v1.1 roadmap — dynamic gate

The v1.1 target is a dynamic gate varying the hold window by claim type. The claim_type signal is already extracted and will be wired into gate logic in v1.1.

08

Known limitations

These limitations are documented as standing constraints of v1.

Data maturity — most outlets below 100 verdicts
Confidence intervals are wide for most outlets. Scores should be interpreted with tier context.
Paywall exclusion
Paywalled articles cannot be accessed for verification, creating systematic underrepresentation of paywalled outlets.
Priority threshold bias
The 30-point threshold filters entertainment and lifestyle content. The verdict pool is intentionally biased toward political and policy claims.
RSS sample representativeness
RSS feeds are a subset of total publishing volume. The verified sample may not be representative of an outlet's full output.
No per-article scores
Verum Signal produces verdict distributions, not per-article scores.
No speaker accuracy records
Speaker accuracy tracking is explicitly out of scope for v1 and not on the v1.x roadmap.
09

Dispute process

Any outlet, journalist, or reader may submit a dispute on any verdict. Disputes are reviewed within 10 business days.

What to include

The specific claim, the verdict assigned, and evidence you believe was missed. Links to primary sources are most useful.

Submit via the correction form on any report page, or at verumsignal.com/disputes.
