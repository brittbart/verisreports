Methodology v1.6
How it works

Three steps, while they're still talking.

From the moment a candidate makes a claim to the moment you see the evidence — usually under a minute.

Step 01

We listen.

Our system transcribes the debate in real time as candidates speak — every word, every speaker, every minute.

Step 02

We extract.

We pull out the specific factual claims — numbers, statistics, policy statements — and separate them from opinion.

Step 03

We check the evidence.

Each claim is checked against independent sources. You see what the evidence says — not our opinion.

What a verdict looks like

Three real examples.
One standard.

Every claim gets a verdict, a one-line summary of what the evidence actually says, and links to the sources we checked. Same template, every speaker, every time.

Mary Holloway · State Senator

"Our teacher pay ranks forty-seventh in the country."

Supported

Lakeland's average starting teacher salary places the state 47th of 50 in the most recent national comparison. Supported by multiple independent education and labor statistics sources.

DR
Daniel Reyes · Former Mayor of Cedar Falls

"We've got the lowest unemployment rate in a generation."

Overstated

Lakeland's unemployment rate is at a multi-year low, but not the lowest on record. State labor data shows the 2018 rate was 0.4 points lower than today's figure.

Based on State Workforce Commission · Monthly Report Federal Labor Statistics

AB
Ada Brennan · Independent candidate

"Violent crime in our capital city has tripled in the last five years."

Not supported

Capital city violent crime is up over the last five years, but by roughly 22% — not 200%. "Tripled" would mean a 200% increase; the actual figure does not approach that threshold.

Where we draw the line

What we don't do.

Some things aren't our job. Calling a winner. Rating a side. Telling you what to think. We don't do any of it — by design.

No. 01

Rate candidates.

We don't score speakers or parties. We analyze individual claims, one at a time, and let the record speak.

No. 02

Tell you who to trust.

The evidence is on the page. The sources are linked. What it means to you — and how it changes your vote — is yours to decide.

No. 03

Hold a political position.

The same standard applies to every claim, from every candidate, in every debate. No exceptions, no thumb on the scale.

We provide the signals. You decide.
